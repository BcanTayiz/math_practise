# Fundamental Mathematics Topics Taught at Universities

This document summarizes the core mathematics subjects commonly taught at universities,
especially in Mathematics, Engineering, Computer Science, and Natural Sciences programs.

---

## 1. Calculus (Mathematical Analysis)

### 1.1 Single-Variable Calculus
- Limits and continuity
- Derivatives and differentiation rules
- Applications of derivatives (optimization, curve sketching)
- Integrals (definite and indefinite)
- Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
- Taylor and Maclaurin series

### 1.2 Multivariable Calculus
- Partial derivatives
- Gradient, divergence, and curl
- Multiple integrals
- Jacobian matrix
- Lagrange multipliers

---

## 2. Linear Algebra

- Vectors and vector spaces
- Matrices and matrix operations
- Systems of linear equations
- Determinants
- Rank of a matrix
- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
- Inner product spaces
- Orthogonality
- Linear transformations

---

## 3. Differential Equations

### 3.1 Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)
- First-order differential equations
- Linear ODEs
- Homogeneous and non-homogeneous equations
- Systems of differential equations
- Phase portraits

### 3.2 Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)
- Heat equation
- Wave equation
- Laplace equation
- Boundary and initial value problems

---

## 4. Discrete Mathematics

- Propositional and predicate logic
- Sets and set operations
- Proof techniques
  - Direct proof
  - Proof by contradiction
  - Mathematical induction
- Combinatorics
- Permutations and combinations
- Recurrence relations
- Graph theory fundamentals

---

## 5. Probability and Statistics

### 5.1 Probability Theory
- Sample spaces and events
- Conditional probability
- Bayes’ theorem
- Random variables
- Expected value
- Variance and covariance

### 5.2 Statistics
- Descriptive statistics
- Probability distributions
  - Normal distribution
  - Binomial distribution
  - Poisson distribution
- Hypothesis testing
- Confidence intervals
- Regression analysis

---

## 6. Numerical Analysis

- Error analysis
- Numerical methods for solving linear systems
- Interpolation and approximation
- Numerical differentiation and integration
- Numerical solutions of differential equations
- Iterative methods

---

## 7. Complex Analysis (Introductory Level)

- Complex numbers
- Analytic functions
- Cauchy–Riemann equations
- Complex integration
- Taylor and Laurent series

---

## 8. Abstract Algebra (Introductory Level)

- Groups
- Rings
- Fields
- Homomorphisms
- Modular arithmetic

---

## 9. Mathematical Logic and Foundations

- Axiomatic systems
- Formal languages
- Truth tables
- Set theory foundations
- Relations and functions

---

## 10. Applied Mathematics Topics

- Optimization
- Operations research
- Game theory (introductory)
- Mathematical modeling

---

## Notes
- The emphasis of topics may vary by department.
- Mathematics programs focus more on abstraction and proofs.
- Engineering and Computer Science programs emphasize applications.
